Why this tool matters

Refinance math should happen before rate shopping gets serious

Borrowers who can already estimate payment impact, break-even timing, and total cost tradeoffs tend to negotiate better and avoid being pulled toward the most marketable quote instead of the most useful quote.

The tool is intentionally simple and mobile-friendly, but it is based on the same fundamental relationships a lender uses when modeling refinance outcomes: balance, rate, term, fees, and equity.

FAQs

Frequently asked refinance questions

What does the pmi removal calculator estimate?

It models a refinance scenario using the figures you enter, then summarizes the payment impact, estimated cost picture, and decision checkpoints most homeowners review before applying.

Are the results a lender quote?

No. The calculator is an educational planning tool. Actual lender quotes depend on credit, occupancy, loan size, state fees, taxes, insurance, reserves, property type, and market conditions on the day you lock.

Should I include taxes and insurance in refinance planning?

Yes. Even when the quoted principal and interest payment falls, the full housing payment can move differently once escrows, mortgage insurance, HOA dues, and prepaid items are considered.
